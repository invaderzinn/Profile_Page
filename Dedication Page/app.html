Chadwick Boseman

November 29, 1976 - August 28, 2020

Actor, Hero, King

Chadwick Aaron Boseman (/ˈboʊzmən/; November 29, 1976 – August 28, 2020) was an American actor and playwrite. After studying directing at Howard University, he became prominent in theater.

Boseman achieved international fame for playing superhero Black Panther in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) from 2016 to 2019. His role as King T'Challa was view by many within the Black Diaspora as pivtiol in the roles Blacks can play on screen and what audience, Black or otherwise, will be exposed to. Many around the world organized events to see the film en masse in theaters. This was done to help cement its success so that movie producers and executives would continue to promote Black/POC talent beyond racial stereotypes.

In 2016, Boseman was diagnosed with colon cancer. Boseman kept his condition private, continuing to act while also extensively supporting cancer charities until his death in August 2020 from the illness. That same year two films, Da Five Bloods and Ma Rainy's Black Bottom, received critcal acclaim at numerous awards ceremonies, the former winning him the Male Actor in a Leading Role award at the 27th Annual Screen Actors Guild Awards.

Chadwick's Timeline

Nov 29, 1976
Chadwick Aaron Boseman is born in Anderson, South Carolina to Carolyn and Leroy Boseman.
1995
Chardwick graduates from from T.L. Hanna High School where he played on the basketball team. He wrote his first play, Crossroads, his junior year. He staged it following the tragic death of a classmate.
1998
Phylicia Rashad becomes his mentor while at Howard. He also meets and is sponsed by Denzel Washington, who helps Chadwick and other classmates to attend the Oxford Summer Progam of the British American Drama Academy at Balliol College, Oxford.
2000
He graduates from Howard University with a Bachelor of Fine Arts in directing. He is named a Drama League Directing Fellow after directing productions such as George C. Wolfe's The Colored Museum and Amri Baraka's Dutchman.
2002
Chadwick works as the drama instructor in the Schomburg Junior Scholars Program in Harlem. He also plays Romeo in Romeo and Juliet and Malcolm in Macbeth, and wins the AUDELCO award for his part in Ron Miller's Urban Transitions.
2002
Writes and directs plays as part of the Hip-hop theater movement. His works include Rhyme Deferred, The Fire This Time, and Hieroglyphinc Graffiti.
2003
He lands his first TV role in an episode of Third Watch. He also begins playing Reggie Mantogmery in the daytime soap opera All My Children. He is fired from this role after voicing concerns on the character's racist sterotypes.
2004
The Congo Square Theatre Company in Chicago commissions his best-known play, Deep Azure. It is later nominated for the Jeff Award for Best New Work in 2006.
2007
He writes, directs, and produdces the short film Blood Over a Broken Pawn, which is honored at the Hollywood Black Film Festival in 2008.
2008
Chadwick turns Deep Azure into a screenplay. Tessa Thompson and Omari Hardwick express interest in the leading roles, prompting Boseman to move to Los Angeles.
2010
Chadwick lands his first regular role in the series Persons Unknown playing marine Graham McNair.
July 2013
Heaven, Chadwick's second short film as a director, premiers at the HollyShorts Film Festival. He also has his breakthough role playing baseball legend Jackie Robinson in 42.
2015
Chadwick stars in another sporting film called Draft Day as fictional football player Vontae Mack. The same year he takes the role of James Brown in the film Get on Up, after being specifically sought out by director Tate Taylor. He also begins dating Taylor Simone Ledward sometime this year.
2016
Chadwick begins portraying the character Black Panther (T'Challa) in the Marvel Cinematic Universe, starring in Captain America: Civil War.
2016
He is diagnosed with stage III solon cancer. This will eventually progress to stage IV sometime before 2020. He never speaks publically about his illness.
2018
He returns as the Black Panther in his own feature film, Black Panther. He becomes one of Time's 100 influential people that year and the film is nominated for an Academy Award for Best Picture.
2017-2020
He stars in several films that receive critical aclaim, including Thurgood Marshall, Da 5 Bloods, and Ma Rainey's Black Bottom.
October 2019
Chardwick reportedly gets engaged to Taylor Simone Ledward and the two later marry in secret.
August 2020
Chadwick Boseman dies at his home in Los Angeles with his wife and family. He was 43 years old.
The tweet from his Twitter account announcing his death becomes the most liked tweet in history, with over 6 million likes in under 24 hours.

Sometimes you need to get knocked down to understand what your fight is.
