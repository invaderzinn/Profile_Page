Experience...

...and Background

I began my coding journey and the the basics of front end development in 2019 from Codecademy. More recently I completed a Web Developer Bootcamp on Udemy in 2022. I gained a lot of knowledge and skills from these courses and have since really enjoyed building my portfolio. Taking what I learned from the Bootcamp, I improved a fictional restaurant page that I started at the beginning of the course. You can find both the old and new restaurant below.

As for my background, I'm sort of a Jack (or Jill) of all trades: I'm a writer (short story, research, screenwriting) and an artist (mostly digital and resin these days). I've also worked in the non-for-profit industry as well as at a law firm as a Proffer and Complaint writer. Suffice it to say I know about pressure and how to keep a deadline!


Most recently I was an English Teacher in Japan for five years, even teaching a basic computer programming class. I gained valuable experience with leadership, planning, and communication. Due to obvious language barriers, you learn to be creative when communicating what is needed. You gotta be willing to get messy if need be, and not stop until you find a solution that works, and then improve on them.


I've found getting messy, making those mistakes, and above all else, learning from them vital in web developing. I'm an avid learner who loves to see progress. I'm currently taking more courses in web development to improve my knowledge base, but am looking to gain real-world experience in the web development job market. I have learned a lot from my coursework, and even things that I have taught myself to do. I'd love the opportunity to gain more experience, hopefully with you.


The projects below were created with Visual Studio Code. I possess skills in HTML, CSS and Javascript, and experience with frameworks like Bootstrap and Bulma. I've also worked with Express.js, Node.js, SQL and No SQL. I have built databases using MongoDB and have used services like Github, Heroku, Cloudinary and MapBox. I'm currently learning React with plans to learn more.
As for human languages, I have a background in Spanish, survival Japanese, and have even dabbled in Hindi and Urdu.
